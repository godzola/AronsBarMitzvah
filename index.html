The part of the Torah I read was about...

The whole thing is about free will. Even if you're not a Jew, or a believer or anything, free will is still a good thing to remember. Having free will means that anytime you can do the right thing or the wrong thing. You can make the right choices or the wrong ones. If you do the right thing, you and hopefully other people will benefit. If you do the worng thing, there will probably be bad outcomes. It might seem complicated but it's really not. Its a good thing to try and be healthy and it's good to try and give something back to people who might be having a hard time right now, or to people who do good things for others.
